Tips to Better Prepare you for Your Interview

Conduct research granularity about the company so you can talk shop

the research is done for you to get familiar with the job and you can start to talk about the job to other people

Research the person with whom you're meeting such as with LinkedIn.

LinkedIn can be used as information about the person that can potentially be interviewing you

Make friends when you walk into the establishment by being super charming to everyone you meet.

anyone you see at the office you need to be very friendly because you dont know who everyone is and who they can be

Keep your answers short but concise. Don't ramble.

keep you answers consistant

Utilize body language with eye contact, posture, way you sit and stand, the way you walk into the room.

Sit up straight and look the interviewer in the eyes dont examine the room be focused.When you walk in greet the person intervieing you and be confedent that you will get the job.https://www.linkedin.com/

Bring a portfolio to keep all interview materials along with predetermined questions.

resume, business cards and a notebook

Have a list of questions to ask.

the interviewer is going to ask you what questinos you have for them at some point of the interview and if you have quesitons it makes it look like you are into the job. Also be prepared to answer any question they may have about you know your positives and your negatives about yourself.

Sell yourself and shine! Don't be modest; however, be confident. Self promote!

This part of the interview is the part where you brag about yourself and you need to make yourself look good

What to wear when going to an interview

When picking your attire pick something depending on the job you are getting interviewed for but you should look one step more formal than everyone else in the office.
